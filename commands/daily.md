# Daily Briefing

Run a daily briefing. Start by getting today's date and day of the week:

```bash
date
```

Then work through each section:

**1. Date and day** — state today's date and what kind of day it is (working day, weekend, etc.)

**2. Open todos** — search your knowledge directories for any files containing todo or task lists. Surface items that are overdue, due today, or flagged as urgent. Keep it to the top 5 — a morning glance, not a full audit.

**3. Priorities for today** — based on what you find in knowledge directories, suggest the top 2–3 things worth focusing on today.

Keep the whole briefing short enough to read in under a minute. If nothing urgent is found, say so clearly.

---
*Customise this command for your setup by editing `~/.claude/commands/daily.md`*
